Global Bid Process

A global, US-led pursuit process that aligns discovery, bid/no-bid decisions, kickoff, solutioning, pricing, and submission across regional sales teams, SMEs, Sales Support, Pricing, CPP, and leadership.

Page owner: Global Sales / Sales Support / Pricing

Process Overview

Use this section as the standard workflow for global opportunities. Each step clarifies ownership, required inputs, collaboration points, and approval expectations before the final response is submitted.

1. Discovery2. Bid / No-Bid3. Kickoff4. Solutioning5. Pricing6. Submit
1

Discovery

Confirm client needs early and bring in the right regional and technology expertise from the start.

  • Sales teams engage regional SMEs as needed to support client Discovery Sessions.
  • A technology consultant is available for technology discovery and CTM global tech stack guidance.
  • Regional SMEs and CPP may be consulted for service configuration solutions.
  • Document client needs, service expectations, technology questions, and solution considerations before moving into bid/no-bid.
2

Bid / No-Bid

Determine whether CTM should submit a proposal based on strategic fit, requirements, profitability, competition, and relationship strength.

  • Complete the WOW with bid/no-bid calculation.
  • Schedule a decision call with the GM, regional sales lead, sales support leads, and relevant SMEs.
  • Include the appropriate CPP representative when a market does not include a CTM wholly-owned agency.
  • Obtain regional lead assignment and distribute bid documents and initial questions to Pricing, Sales Support, and regional leads.
3

Kickoff

Once the bid is approved, align the pursuit team on scope, ownership, expectations, and target dates.

  • Hold a project kickoff with the GM, regional leads, Pricing, Sales Support, and CTM M&E if applicable.
  • Use the WOW so participants can review key information before the call.
  • Review customer overview, relationship status, goals and solutions, win strategy, client requirements, risks, pricing strategy, and target dates.
  • Confirm owners, action items, deadlines, and escalation points.
4

Solutioning

Build and approve the global service solution with the right regional, technology, and partner agency input.

  • Work with regional SMEs and the technology consultant to devise the global service solution.
  • Regional sales leads devise and approve the service solution within their region.
  • If service falls under a CTM Partner agency, work with the appropriate CPP representative to determine the partner-agency approach.
  • Store the WOW in Sales SharePoint as a continuously updated global company shared version.
  • After the proposed solution is formulated, secure regional GM approval and share the configuration with Sales Support and Pricing.
5

Pricing

Coordinate global pricing alignment, cost model completion, approvals, and final commercial review.

  • Collaborate with the pricing team to prepare and finalize required documentation.
  • Submit cost model components, the WOW, client-required sales documents, assumptions, and timelines to Pricing; allow a 10-day turnaround.
  • Arrange a strategy meeting with regional sales leaders, the global pricing lead, and Sales Support to address open questions.
  • The deal captain develops the pricing strategy with EVP Business Development and SVP Sales, then aligns with regional leadership.
  • Pricing returns the vetted pricing and strategy for final review and confirmation.
  • Please note: Any bid valued over $20 million requires CEO approval before submission.
6

Submit

Finalize the response package, confirm approvals, and submit the global RFP response.

  • Sales Support partners with Sales to complete the questionnaire, executive summary, and required attachments.
  • Sales reviews and approves draft versions before final submission.
  • The final response is built using promised documentation, attachments, and any required regional or SME inputs.
  • Submit through the required portal or client delivery method and retain the final copy in SharePoint.
  • After submission, orchestrate global presentations and technology demos with regional SMEs and CTM Partner agencies through APP as needed.

Suggested SharePoint Setup

Recommended location: Global Sales > Global Bid Process. Store the WOW, bid/no-bid notes, kickoff summary, pricing files, solution configuration, final response, and presentation/demo materials in the opportunity folder.

Recommended metadata: Opportunity Name, Region, Deal Captain, Regional Lead, Sales Support Owner, Pricing Owner, Status, Due Date, Approval Required, CEO Approval Required, and Final Submission Link.

North America Bid Process

Use this Domestic, US-led process to decide whether to pursue the opportunity, align the right owners, finalize pricing, and submit a complete response.

Bid/No-Bid

Assess whether the client aligns with CTM offerings before pursuing a bid. Review the bid, understand client needs, and confirm whether CTM can deliver profitably.

  • Complete the WOW with bid/no-bid calculation.
  • Distribute bid documents and initial questions to GM, regional sales lead, and Sales Support.
  • Schedule the decision call with GM, regional sales lead, Sales Support leads, and SMEs.
  • Request Finance to run the D&B report and confirm pass/fail viability.

Kickoff

Once the bid is approved to proceed, hold a project kickoff with everyone needed to complete the bid components.

  • GM, regional leads, Pricing, Sales Support, and CTM M&E if applicable.
  • Review WOW inputs and the high-level bid approach.
  • Confirm customer overview, relationship status, goals, win strategy, requirements, risks, pricing strategy, and target dates.

Pricing

Submit cost model components, required sales documents, assumptions, and timelines to the pricing team.

  • Allow a 10-day turnaround whenever possible.
  • BAS reviews the internal summary and opportunity details.
  • Pricing, profitability, and walk-in/walk-out strategy are aligned with EVP/SVP as needed.
  • Final pricing is provided to Sales Support for the submission package.

Submit

Sales Support partners with the sales lead to complete the questionnaire, executive summary if appropriate, and all required attachments.

  • Sales lead reviews draft response for accuracy.
  • Finalize all response documents, pricing, and references.
  • Upload to the portal or send to client by the assigned owner.
  • Coordinate post-submission presentations, clarifications, and tech demos.

To bid, or Not to bid

Not every client aligns with what CTM offers, so it is critical to assess whether pursuing the bid is worth the time and effort. Once a bid is received, the first step is to read every requirement carefully to understand the client’s needs.

Next, evaluate overall compatibility by asking:

  • Can CTM offer a solution that fits the client’s goals?
  • Do we meet the client’s basic requirements?
  • Is there an existing relationship with the client?
  • Can we fulfill the client’s needs profitably?
  • Has Finance confirmed D&B customer viability?

Complete as much of the WOW as possible and fill in the Go/No-Go tab to support a strategic decision. Then schedule a call with the regional general manager, Sales Support leads, and either the EVP or VP of Sales to determine whether to proceed or decline.

Confirm need, interest, goals, pain points, and whether there is a real opportunity to pursue.

Align stakeholders, build the right solution, and prepare the proposal or RFP response.

Key players engaged, solution aligned, buying process understood.

20%Relationship map
4

Proposal Submitted

Proposal, RFP/RFI response, or proactive proposal submitted.

0–30%Proposal submitted

Present the solution, address clarifications, review pricing, and move toward award.

Stages 5–7
5

Presentation Stage

40%Presentation
6

50–85%Negotiation
7

Verbal Award

Verbal approval granted and written intent or redlining underway.

95%Intent to award

Phase 4: Close and Learn

Close the opportunity, document the outcome, and capture win/loss feedback.

Stage 8
8

Contract signed, formal loss received, no decision, or no activity closure.

0–100%Outcome documented
