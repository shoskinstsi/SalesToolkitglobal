Global Bid Process

A clear, role-based pursuit process that aligns discovery, bid/no-bid decisions, kickoff, solutioning, pricing, approvals, and final submission across global and regional teams.

Page owner: Global Sales / Sales Support / Pricing

How to use this process

Start with the stage of the pursuit, confirm the owner and required inputs, then use the related output to move the opportunity forward. This view is designed to reduce ambiguity during active global bids.

6-step workflow
Need a template?Open bid resources, WOW, executive briefing, pricing, and response files. Need an owner?Open RACI to confirm responsible, accountable, consulted, and informed teams. Need support?Open Help & Contacts to route bid questions to the correct support area. Bid complete?After the decision, complete the win/loss debrief and capture lessons learned.
Executive reminder: Any bid valued over $20 million requires CEO approval before submission. Use the process below to confirm approval needs early and avoid late-stage delays.
1

Discovery

Confirm client needs and bring the right regional, service, and technology expertise into the conversation early.

Primary actionEngage regional SMEs, CPP, and technology consultants as needed for discovery sessions.
Required inputClient requirements, service expectations, technology questions, regional needs, and known risks.
OutputDocumented discovery notes that support the bid/no-bid decision.
2

Bid / No-Bid

Decide whether CTM should pursue based on fit, requirements, profitability, competition, and relationship strength.

Primary actionComplete the WOW and bid/no-bid calculation.
Required inputGM, regional sales lead, Sales Support, SMEs, Pricing, and CPP input where applicable.
OutputDocumented decision, regional lead assignment, and approved next steps.
3

Kickoff

Align the pursuit team on scope, ownership, deadlines, strategy, risks, and approval expectations.

Primary actionHold a formal kickoff with the GM, regional leads, Pricing, Sales Support, and CTM M&E if applicable.
Required inputWOW, client overview, win strategy, requirements, pricing approach, and target dates.
OutputConfirmed action log, owners, deadlines, escalation points, and review cadence.
4

Solutioning

Build and approve the global service solution with regional, technology, and partner agency input.

Primary actionDefine the service model, regional approach, technology stack, and CPP or partner agency path.
Required inputRegional SME recommendations, technology discovery, service configuration, and partner agency guidance.
OutputApproved service solution shared with Sales Support and Pricing.
5

Pricing

Coordinate pricing strategy, cost model completion, assumptions, and final commercial review.

Primary actionSubmit cost model components, WOW, client documents, assumptions, and timelines to Pricing.
Required inputRegional pricing needs, open questions, approval requirements, and deal captain strategy.
OutputVetted pricing and strategy returned for final review and confirmation.
6

Submit

Finalize the response package, confirm approvals, submit to the client, and retain the final record.

Primary actionSales Support and Sales complete the questionnaire, executive summary, final response, and attachments.
Required inputFinal pricing, solution approvals, regional inputs, SME materials, and client portal requirements.
OutputSubmitted response, final archive in SharePoint, and presentation or demo follow-up plan.

SharePoint Setup and Governance

Recommended LocationGlobal Sales > Global Bid Process with one opportunity folder for each active pursuit.
Required FilesWOW, bid/no-bid notes, kickoff summary, pricing files, solution configuration, final response, and presentation/demo materials.
Recommended MetadataOpportunity Name, Region, Deal Captain, Sales Support Owner, Pricing Owner, Status, Due Date, Approval Required, CEO Approval Required, and Final Submission Link.
